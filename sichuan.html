Lahpet thoke is the name of the famous fermented tea leaf salad from Myanmar, a country that is really known for its very unique, healthy and fragrant salads. There are two main traditional ways to consume pickled tea leaf or lahpet. The first one as a meal or tea leaf salad called lahpet thoke and the second one as a main snack called ahlu lahpet.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried green tea leaves
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 3 scallions finely chopped
  • 1 bunch cilantro (coriander leaf)
  • 2 cloves garlic , crushed
  • Zest of 2 limes
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 cup vegetable oil

Preparation

  1. Trim 2 inches (5 cm) from each stalk, thinly slice, and set aside. Pound the remaining stalks and cut into 3-inch (8 cm) sections.
  2. Boil 10 cups (2.5 L) water with dashi powder and 2 tbsp fish sauce. Add pounded lemongrass and fish fillets. Simmer 10 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet. Saut√© onion, garlic, ginger, and sliced lemongrass for 3-4 minutes. Add turmeric, paprika, 1 tbsp fish sauce, and salt. Cook 5 minutes.
  4. Add cooked fish to the skillet. Stir over medium-high heat for 10 minutes. Add 4 tbsp fish sauce, rice flour, and stir. Simmer until thickened, about 15 minutes.
  5. Serve noodles in bowls, cover with broth, add half a boiled egg, cilantro, and crushed pepper. Garnish with lime.
